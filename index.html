    • Overview

  • A primary research was conducted on 400 students across 17 departments at Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST).
  • Aimed to uncover the factors influencing students’ participation in freelancing and tuition activities.

    • Methodology

  • Employed Probability Proportional to Size (PPS) and Simple Random Sampling (SRS) techniques to ensure representative sampling of 400 students across 17 departments.
  • Analysis involved both descriptive statistics and inferential methods, including chi-square tests and binary logistic regression modeling, to examine the relationships between students’ background characteristics and their involvement in such activities.

    • Findings

  • Significant associations between factors such as academic performance, financial background, and the likelihood of engaging in freelancing or tuition.
  • Students from lower-income families were more likely to be involved in these activities as a means of financial support.
